  • Five Strategies for Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) Modernization

    Slow and complex legacy EDI software used for communication and collaboration among trading partners is a major roadblock to digital transformation. Legacy EDI doesn’t provide the speed, simplicity, and ease of use that businesses need today. It’s time to modernize.

  • Enable Distributed Deployment

    Simply migrating EDI to the cloud is not enough. Make sure you have the flexibility to deploy your EDI runtime anywhere with cloud-native, distributed deployment capabilities.

  • Create End-to-End Business Visibility

    Leverage dashboards that span the many EDI transactions that make a single “order-to-cash” process and translate EDI protocols and formatting into something easily understood, so business users can see the full picture and diagnose issues themselves.

  • Ensure Seamless Flow to Your ERP

    Deploy a strategy that doesn’t require you to use one internal application integration solution for passing EDI data into your ERP applications and then another solution for workflow automation and data management.

  • Implement Self-Service Onboarding

    Consider complementing EDI with new methodologies that can contribute new capabilities to your B2B services overall. Ensure your integration platform that includes EDI also supports future requirements such as workflow automation, data management, and more.
